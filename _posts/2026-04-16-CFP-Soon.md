SeaGL 2026 is coming together, and we have a handful of exciting updates to share with the community. We are so excited to let you know that our Call for Proposals is opening next week! 🎉

## 📢 Call for Proposals opens April 24th

If you have a talk, workshop, or session idea that you have been sitting on, now is the time to dust it off: the SeaGL 2026 Call for Proposals opens on **April 24th**. SeaGL has always been a conference that welcomes speakers at every level of experience, from folks sharing something publicly for the very first time to seasoned presenters with years of stage time behind them. If you have something to say, we want to hear it.

We will share full details about the CFP process, including topics, formats, and submission guidelines, when it opens. In the meantime, start thinking about what you know, what you are passionate about, and what you think the SeaGL community would benefit from hearing. We cannot wait to see what you submit. 🐧

## Come find us at LinuxFest Northwest

The SeaGL team will be on the ground at [LinuxFest Northwest](https://linuxfestnorthwest.org/) with a booth, and if you are there too, please stop by and give us a squak.

It is a wonderful opportunity to meet some of the people who put SeaGL together each year, ask questions about how the conference works, find out how to get involved, or simply spend a few minutes talking about the free and open-source tools we all care about. Community events like LinuxFest Northwest are a big part of what keeps the FLOSS ecosystem vibrant, and we are glad to be a part of it. 💚

## Help us choose our keynote speakers

Keynotes set the tone for the entire conference, and we think the SeaGL community is in the best position to help us get them right. This year, we are asking attendees, past speakers, volunteers, and anyone who cares about SeaGL to nominate someone they think would make a meaningful and inspiring keynote 🎤 speaker.

Think about the people whose work you admire, whose advocacy has made a difference, or whose perspective you think would challenge and energize an audience of free and open source software enthusiasts. We reserve half the keynote positions for first-time keynoters, nominees do not need to be widely known; they just need to have something they are passionate about sharing in FLOSS and a connection to the values that SeaGL is built on.

Go to [Keynote Nomination](/keynote_suggestions) to submit a nomination. 🙌 The SeaGL team will review all nominations as we plan the program.

## Keep in touch

There is more news on the way as SeaGL 2026 takes shape. Follow us on social media and keep an eye on this blog for updates on the CFP, the program, and everything else we have in store. If you are interested in volunteering or getting more involved with the organizing effort, visit our [Get Involved](/get_involved) page or fill out our [volunteer application form](/volunteer).

Thank you, as always, for being part of what makes SeaGL special. We are looking forward to a wonderful 2026, and we hope to see you there. 🐦
